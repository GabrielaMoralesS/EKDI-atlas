Data Integrity & Validation

EKDI results are only useful if data quality, uncertainty and biome-specific calibration are visible.

Occurrence IntegrityValid coordinates, event dates, basis of record, duplicate checks and coordinate uncertainty.
Taxonomic IntegrityAccepted names, synonym resolution, taxon match evidence support and voucher review flags.
Landscape IntegrityLand-cover completeness, temporal coverage, cloud/no-data flags and spatial alignment.
Model IntegrityWeight sensitivity, evidence completeness classes, expert calibration and validation against known sites.

Weight Transfer Rule

The Atlantic Forest pilot parametrization should not be copied blindly to another biome. EKDI treats weights as calibration parameters: run sensitivity analysis, compare rankings against known survey sites, and document expert adjustments before publishing a new atlas.

Suggested Validation Checks

Compare top-ranked cells with known recent botanical surveys.
Hold out a subset of verified sites and test whether EKDI ranks them as lower uncertainty.
Test whether top priority cells remain stable under ±10–20% weight changes.
Ask local taxonomic or herbarium experts to review a sample of ghost and rediscovery candidates.
