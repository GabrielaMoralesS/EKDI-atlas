# GBIF Submission Notes

## Project Title

EKDI Atlas — Ecological Knowledge Decay Index

## Short Description

EKDI is a field-verification atlas that connects GBIF-mediated biodiversity records with land-cover change to identify where ecological knowledge may need botanical review.

## Problem

Biodiversity records preserve evidence of life at a place and time, but landscapes may change after records are collected.

Researchers need transparent tools to identify where old records, habitat change and knowledge gaps should trigger renewed field verification.

## Approach

EKDI combines sampling antiquity, post-record forest loss and richness deficit into an interpretable priority index.

The dashboard then connects spatial priority to botanical evidence, Knowledge Ghosts, Rediscovery hypotheses and expedition outputs.

## Added Value

- Transparent scoring.
- Field-verification priorities.
- Botanical Memory profiles.
- Knowledge Ghosts.
- Rediscovery hypotheses.
- Expedition outputs.
- Replicable workflow for other biomes.

## Open Science

- Uses open biodiversity data.
- Exports static files.
- Designed for reproducibility.
- Documents limitations and caveats.
- Supports local adaptation and recalibration.

## What EKDI Does Not Do

- Does not claim extinction.
- Does not confirm presence.
- Does not replace expert review.
- Does not replace field verification.
